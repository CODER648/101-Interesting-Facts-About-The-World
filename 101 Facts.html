The 101 Facts

1-10

1. In 2005, a fortune cookie company called Wonton Food Inc. correctly foretold lottery numbers, resulting in 110 winners and an investigation. Surprisingly, no fraud was involved.
2. In South Korea there is an emergency number (113) to report spies.
3. Mantis Shrimps have the strongest sense of colour in the animal kingdom.
4. North Korea and Cuba are the only places you can't buy Coca-Cola.
5. The entire world's population could fit inside Los Angeles. (If all 7.5 billion people stood shoulder-to-shoulder, they could all fit within the 500 square miles of Los Angeles)
6. The Canary Islands are named after dogs, not birds.
7. The longest place name on the planet is 85 letters long. (Taumatawhakatangihanga-koauauotamateaturipukakapikimaung-ahoronukupokaiwhenuakitanatahu, New Zealand)
8. Four babies are born every second.
9. There are only three countries in the world that don't use the metric system.
10. The coldest temperature ever recorded was -144 degrees Fahrenheit. (Just a few breaths of air at that temperature would kill you)

11-20

11. Japan is the world's most earthquake-prone country.
12. There are around 4 quadrillion quadrillion bacteria on Earth.
13. People who are currently alive represent about 7 percent of the total number of people who have ever lived.
14. Only two countries use purple in their national flags. (The flag of Nicaragua has a rainbow in the center that includes a band of purple, and the flag of Dominica has a picture of a bird with purple feathers.)
15. The most expensive coin in the world was sold for more than $7 million.
16. South Sudan is the youngest country in the world. (South Sudan in North Africa just gained its independence from Sudan in 2011, which currently makes it the youngest country in the world.)
17. People 60 years and older make up 12.3 percent of the global population.
18. There are more than 24 time zones around the world.
19. Nearly half of the world's population watched both the 2010 and 2014 FIFA World Cup games.
20. There are 43 countries that still have a royal family.

21-30

21. All giant pandas in zoos around the world are on loan from China.
22. Canada has nine percent of the world's forests.
23. More people speak Mandarin Chinese than any other language.
24. Around one in every 200 men (16 million people) are direct descendants of Genghis Khan.
25. Copenhagen is the most bike-friendly city in the world.
26. Facebook has more users than the population of the U.S., China, and Brazil combined.
27. There are 41 countries that recognize sign language as an official language.
28. The oceans contain almost 200,000 different kinds of viruses.
29. New Zealanders have more pets per household than any other country.
30. Tokyo is the world's largest city. It has 37 million inhabitants.

31-40

31. Nearly two people die each second (Four people are born each second).
32. There is a McDonald’s in every continent except Antartica.
33. Most toilet paper sold for home use in France is pink.
34. Bullfrogs do not sleep.
35. The dark region on the north pole of Pluto’s moon, Charon, is called Mordor (Like J.R.R Tolkien's Lord Of The Rings's Mordor).
36. Eight of the ten largest statues in the world are of Buddhas.
37. In 2015, a silver coin with Superman on the heads side was made which is legal tender in Canada. There was only 350,000 produced.
38. Sunsets on Mars are blue.
39. All dogs are banned from Antarctica since April 1994. This ban was made because of concern that dogs might spread diseases to seals.
40. AIn 1999 hackers revealed a security flaw in Hotmail that permitted anybody to log into any Hotmail account using the password ‘eh’. At the time it was called “the most widespread security incident in the history of the web”. Fortunately, they have fixed that problem now.

41-50

41. If you made $1 every second, it would take you 2,921 years to have more money than Bill Gates (over $92.1 billion dollars).
42. Big Ben (Elizabeth Tower) in London is leaning over so much it can now be seen with the naked eye. In 4,000 years it will be at the same angle as the tower in Pisa is now.
43. Australia has over 10,000 beaches. You could visit a new beach everyday for over 27 years!
44. The mayor of the Alaskan town, Talkeetna, is called Stubbs and has been mayor of the town since July 1997 until his death in 2017. Stubbs is a cat.
45. The Nazis were the first ever people in modern history to start an anti-smoking and tobacco movement.
46. There is an uninhabited island in the Bahamas known as Pig Beach, which is populated entirely by swimming pigs.
47. Artist Salvador Dali would often get out of paying for drinks and meals by drawing on the checks, making them priceless works of art and therefore un-cashable.
48. Hawaii‘s state flag is the only US state flag to feature the Union Jack upon it.
49. Approximately 1,000,000 dogs in the U.S. are named as the heirs of their owners’ wills.
50. When written down, the word ‘almost’ is the longest word in the English language to have all of its letters in alphabetical order.

51-60

51. In 2012, an Afghan Taliban Commander Mohammed Ashan turned himself in to local authorities, trying to claim the $100 reward prize he had seen on a poster for his arrest.
52. The country of Lichtenstein has an extremely low crime rate, and according to one member of their police there hasn’t been a murder in Lichtenstein since the late 1990’s! Lichtenstein’s entire Police Force consists of 91 officers and 34 civilian staff just 125 in total.
53. France has conducted 210 nuclear weapon tests, more than the United Kingdom, China, India, and North Korea combined! This is scarcely a fifth of the amount conducted by the United States, however, who have conducted roughly 1,054 tests.
54. The world’s biggest ever recorded turtle was a Leatherback Turtle that washed up Harlech Beach, Wales, in 1988. It was estimated to be 100 years old, and was almost 9 foot in length weighing a tumultuous 2,016 lbs!
55. During the First World War, so many starving wolves had amassed together in a great pack that opposing German and Russian forces formed a temporary alliance in order to fight them off!
56. The least financially successful Harry Potter film made approximately $90 million more than the most successful Twilight movie.
57. In mid 2013, an earthquake-proof church was constructed in Christchurch, New Zealand, using mainly cardboard as the primary building material! Constructed from 98 giant cardboard tubes coated in multiple layers of waterproof polyurethane the ‘cardboard cathedral’ cost $6 million to build and is designed to last for up to 50 years!
58. The bumblebee bat is not only the world’s smallest bat, but it is also the world’s smallest mammal. These cute little creatures weigh only 2 grams. They are 29 to 33mm in length, with a wingspan of approximately 170mm!
59. In 2007, a 1000-gallon inflatable swimming pool was stolen from someone’s back-garden without a single drop of water being found!
60. Russia has more surface area than Pluto.

61-70

61. There is no specific time zone at the South Pole. This is because all the longitude lines on the planet meet up there.
62. The first ever 3D film was released in cinemas in 1922.
63. There was a type of Pterodactyl with a bigger wingspan than a fighter jet.
64. The Burj Khalifa is so tall you can see two sunsets from it in one day. (You can see the sunset happen at ground level, and then if you get the elevator all the way up the building you can see it set again from the top)
65. High heels were originally men’s shoes.
66. There is a smoke alarm for the deaf.
67. Armadillos have shells so hard they can deflect a bullet.
68. Only a quarter of the Sahara Desert is sandy.
69. Antarctica is the world’s largest desert (The Sahara is roughly 3.6 million square miles while the Antarctic Polar Desert covers the Antarctica continent and covers roughly 5.5 million square miles).
70. The Eiffel Tower “grows” in the summer. (Due to the heat expansion of the tower’s iron in the summer sun, the Eiffel Tower’s height can grow by up to 6 inches!)

71-80

71. Bees can fly higher than Mount Everest!
72. In 1997 a cargo ship lost 4.8 million Lego bits in a storm. They are still washing up today.
73. The smallest dinosaur ever discovered is only 16 inches long.
74. The letter ‘E’ is the most common letter in the English language. It appears in roughly 11% of all words used in the English language.
75. New Zealand is actually part of a much bigger, sunken landmass.
76. Koalas have unique fingerprints. Koala fingerprints are so similar to human fingerprints that even the most seasoned forensic scientists would have a hard time telling a koala fingerprint from a human one.
77. There are sharks which can live for up to 500 years. (Greenland Sharks have the longest known lifespan of all vertebrate animals)
78. All astronauts going to the International Space Station have to learn how to speak Russian. This is because astronauts traveling to the ISS must hitch a ride with Russian cosmonauts, and the controls of their Soyuz spacecraft are in Russian. The first British astronaut to go to the ISS, Commander Tim Peake said “Learning Russian has been the single most difficult aspect of my training”!
79. Sharks are older than trees.
80. Scientists in California created a machine to harness power from snowfall.

81-90

81. An ant can’t die from falling. (They could even survive being dropped from heights like the Empire State Building and walk away unscathed)
82. Before trees existed, Earth was covered with giant white mushrooms.
83. Soviet Cosmonauts took shotguns to space with them. This wasn’t to fight off any capitalist aliens they might encounter in space… it was for when they returned to Earth in case they landed in Siberia and had to fend off hungry bears.
84. Dogs can learn up to 250 words and gestures.
85. 40 million years ago penguins were 6 feet tall.
86. 25% of all mammal species on Earth are bats (There are a whopping 1,200 different types of bat!).
87. Fortune cookies are an American invention.
88. Italy has 34 native languages in use today.
89. Amsterdam’s Royal Palace sits on 13,659 wooden poles.
90. There is a gargoyle with Darth Vader’s head on it on the Washington Cathedral.

91-100

91. Two stealth nuclear submarines once bumped into each other by accident (They were both cloaked so well from each other that neither submarine could detect the other, even when they were only a couple feet apart).
92. NASA’s internet speed is 91 GB per second! (This means on NASA’s internet you could download a high-quality 1080p movie in mere milliseconds!)
93. Roughly half a human body is red blood cells.
94. Malta has never experienced weather below freezing point.
95. In 1783, a volcanic eruption killed 80% of the Earth’s sheep.
The Laki volcanic fissure in the South of Iceland erupted over an eight-month period back in 1783, releasing some straight-up nasty hydrogen fluoride into the atmosphere to shower down on the Earth’s inhabitants. Because of this, roughly 25% of the world’s human population died.
96. The US has more millionaires than Sweden has people (There are over 10 million millionaires in the US, whilst Sweden has a population of less than 10 million people).
97. Camels don’t actually store water in their humps - they store fat!
98. The only letter that doesn’t appear on the periodic table is J.
99. At birth, a baby panda is smaller than a mouse.
100. Iceland does not have a railway system.
